Brain Tumor Cancer Prediction

Abstract

Brain tumors are among the most serious and life-threatening medical conditions, necessitating rapid and accurate diagnosis to improve patient outcomes. Traditional diagnostic methods can be time-consuming and often require expert interpretation. In recent years, machine learning (ML) has shown significant promise in automating and enhancing the accuracy of medical diagnoses, including brain tumor classification. This study explores the application of machine learning algorithms to classify brain tumors based on imaging data, focusing on key types such as gliomas, meningiomas, and pituitary tumors. A dataset of MRI images is utilized, and pre-processing techniques are applied to improve the quality and consistency of the data. Several ML models, including convolutional neural networks (CNNs), support vector machines (SVM), and k-nearest neighbors (KNN), are implemented and evaluated. Our experiments demonstrate that CNNs outperform traditional ML models in accurately classifying tumor types, with a particular emphasis on feature extraction and spatial analysis inherent to MRI images. The results indicate the potential for machine learning-based systems to assist healthcare professionals by providing fast, reliable, and cost-effective tumor classification. This research highlights the advantages of ML in medical image analysis and underscores the importance of further studies to integrate such models into clinical workflows.
